Lilongwe Water Board Treatment Works III

Part of the team that design a new 50,000m3/day water treatment plant for the Lilongwe Water Board, Malawi.

Main Roles

MEICA Design

Responsible for all mechanical and electrical aspects of the design realting to the water treatment process.

What I did

  • Hydraulic analysis.
  • Pumps/Instrumentation.
  • Process analysis.
  • Power analysis.

Hydraulic Analysis

Pipe sizing, site-wide hydraulic profiles, waste water management system.

Sized all pipework on site and liased with senior CAD technicans on the pipe layout. Devloped a comprehensive hydraulic profile of the treatment to check the headlosses and gravity fed flows throughout the entire treatment works. Designed a waste management system for the site to handle the sludge from the presedimentation ad sedminetation processes, emergency overflows and wastewater from the filter backwashing.

Pumps/ Instrumentations

Sizing, control philsophy.

Preapred specifications for all the pumps needs for the treatment works and consulted with local suppliers to choose the approiate model. Worked alongside the senior MEICA engineer to select the sensors to be used on site and develop a site wide control philsophy.

Process Analysis

Water qulaity data processing, process reviews, chemical CAPEX/OPEX comparison study.

Analysed and processed 10 years of daily water quality readings from the existing plants. Using this data apprpriate water treatment processes were selected. Preformed detailed cost benefit analysi of serval treatment processes in consultataion with local chemcial suppliers.

Power Analysis

Site power, backup generators, renewable energy audit.

Sized and selected the transformer and backup power generator to be installed onsite. Performance a solar power potenial study for the site and prepsred a report to be sent to the client for consideration.

Documentation

Preparation of tender documents and detailed technical specifications.

Prepared documentation for the project and inception, preliminary design and detailed design stages.
